# Rubric

## Short paragraph rubric: 10 points

| Dimension | Points | What to look for |
|---|---:|---|
| Core judgment is clear | 0-2 | The first or second sentence says what the paragraph believes, not merely what happened. |
| Logic chain is complete | 0-2 | The paragraph does not jump from abstract cause to grand conclusion. |
| Concrete mechanism or example exists | 0-2 | The paragraph names actions such as compile, test, inspect logs, migrate dependencies, fix macros, rerun CI. |
| Language avoids empty grandeur | 0-2 | Big words are either absent or grounded in concrete objects/actions. |
| Personal voice is preserved | 0-2 | The paragraph keeps the user's sharp口语感 when useful. |

## Essay rubric: 20 points

| Dimension | Points | What to look for |
|---|---:|---|
| Thesis | 0-3 | The article has one clear controlling judgment. |
| Structure | 0-3 | Sections follow a visible sequence: phenomenon, problem, mechanism, consequence, boundary. |
| Mechanism | 0-4 | The article explains how the change happens, not only that it happens. |
| Evidence | 0-3 | Cases, numbers, realistic technical actions, or named systems support the claim. |
| Boundaries | 0-3 | The article states what the claim does not imply. |
| Language | 0-2 | Sentences are clear, not bloated. |
| Style | 0-2 | Personal voice is present but not distracting. |

## Teacher feedback format

For each review, provide:

1. Core judgment extracted.
2. Logic chain reconstructed.
3. Main jump or missing link.
4. Abstract words to replace.
5. Sentence worth keeping.
6. Score.
7. One revision target.
8. Model revision.
9. Second-pass assignment.
