# Glossary

## Core judgment

A sentence that states what the writer believes the event means. It is not a description of the event.

## Logic chain

The sequence that connects claim to consequence.

Example:

```text
成本下降 → 更多人进入 → 基础能力商品化 → 竞争焦点上移
```

## Mechanism

The process by which a claim becomes true. In technical writing, mechanism often means tools, feedback loops, actions, and constraints.

## De-abstracting

The act of replacing a big word with concrete actions, actors, objects, or failure modes.

## Personal punchline

A vivid phrase that compresses a paragraph's conclusion. It works only after the reasoning has been built.

## Zone of proximal development

The next task that is slightly difficult but still doable with feedback. In this workspace, it is inferred from learning records and recent submissions.
