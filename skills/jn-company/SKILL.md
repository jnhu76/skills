# Company Generation Screener

## Purpose

This skill helps analyze a listed company or private company through a Chinese industrial development framework.

The goal is not to decide whether to buy or sell immediately.

The goal is to determine:

* which industrial generation the company belongs to;
* whether it has real generation 6 / 7 / 8 characteristics;
* whether it is a true technology / globalization / resource-control company;
* whether it is just using AI, self-reliance, globalization, or new-quality-productivity narratives;
* whether it deserves further investment research.

The core principle:

> A company is not judged by what story it tells, but by whether its capability has turned into revenue, margin, cash flow, pricing power, supply-chain control, or geopolitical resilience.

---

# 1. Industrial Generation Framework

Use the following generation model as an analytical lens.

## Generation 1: Private Business / Market Opening

Core capability:

* entrepreneurship;
* market instinct;
* willingness to take risk;
* early private-sector growth.

Typical slogan:

> Dare to think, dare to act.

Main risk:

* weak legal protection;
* weak technology;
* vulnerable to policy and institutional changes.

---

## Generation 2: Technology and Resource Introduction

Core capability:

* importing foreign technology;
* importing capital;
* learning global business rules;
* assembling supply chains;
* using international resources.

Typical slogan:

> International vision.

Main risk:

* dependency on foreign technology;
* weak original IP;
* vulnerability to sanctions and licensing restrictions.

---

## Generation 3: Internet Platform

Core capability:

* traffic aggregation;
* network effects;
* platform operations;
* data-driven user growth;
* consumer internet monetization.

Typical slogan:

> Network era.

Main risk:

* regulatory pressure;
* traffic saturation;
* platform rent-seeking;
* limited hard-technology depth.

---

## Generation 4: Real Estate / Urbanization

Core capability:

* land finance;
* leverage;
* construction;
* urban infrastructure expansion;
* household balance-sheet expansion.

Typical slogan:

> Give you a home.

Main risk:

* debt cycle;
* demographic pressure;
* asset bubble;
* policy deleveraging.

---

## Generation 5: Application and Technology Landing

Core capability:

* product definition;
* engineering integration;
* supply-chain coordination;
* user experience;
* commercialization;
* turning existing technology into usable products.

Typical slogan:

> Make technology usable.

Main risk:

* weak core technology;
* relying on imported components;
* high competition;
* margin compression.

---

## Generation 6: Independent Intellectual Property

Core capability:

* original technology;
* key patents;
* core components;
* self-controlled supply chain;
* domestic substitution;
* resistance to foreign sanctions;
* technology that directly improves product, cost, or margin.

Typical slogan:

> Self-reliance and self-strengthening.

Main test:

> If foreign supply, license, equipment, or software access is restricted, can the company still produce, iterate, and compete?

Main risk:

* fake self-reliance narrative;
* patent quantity without product power;
* R&D spending without commercial conversion;
* technology that does not improve margin or customer dependence.

---

## Generation 7: Global Expansion

Core capability:

* overseas revenue;
* overseas channels;
* overseas brands;
* overseas compliance;
* overseas manufacturing;
* global supply-chain management;
* ability to survive tariffs, anti-subsidy investigations, and geopolitical pressure.

Typical slogan:

> Open international markets.

Main test:

> Can the company earn sustainable overseas profit, not just export volume?

Main risk:

* export without localization;
* overseas revenue without overseas margin;
* geopolitical pressure;
* tariffs;
* compliance risk;
* brand weakness;
* relying only on low price.

---

## Generation 8: Chinese International Capital / Global Resource Allocation

Core capability:

* global capital deployment;
* resource control;
* mining rights;
* energy assets;
* ports;
* logistics;
* overseas production networks;
* global standards;
* long-term contracts;
* ability to bring global resources back into the Chinese industrial system.

Typical slogan:

> Bring global resources home.

Main test:

> Does the company control scarce global resources, channels, production capacity, standards, or strategic infrastructure?

Main risk:

* political risk;
* currency risk;
* expropriation risk;
* sanctions;
* overseas asset impairment;
* unstable local partners.

## Category A: Technology That Improves Cost or Margin

* cost structure;
* yield;
* efficiency;
* product performance;
* gross margin;
* customer stickiness;
* supply-chain security.

* technology only appears in presentations;

# 6. Anti-Narrative Filter

For any company using the following narratives, apply strict verification.

## AI Narrative

Do not accept:

* “AI-enabled”
* “large model”
* “intelligent platform”
* “AI ecosystem”

unless it changes:

* revenue;
* margin;
* user retention;
* paid services;
* cost reduction;
* product competitiveness.

Ask:

```text
Where does AI appear in the income statement?
Where does AI appear in the balance sheet?
Where does AI appear in cash flow?
Are customers paying for it?
```

---

## Self-Reliance Narrative

Do not accept:

* “domestic substitution”
* “independent IP”
* “full-stack self-developed”
* “core technology”

unless it changes:

* supply security;
* product performance;
* gross margin;
* customer dependence;
* replacement of foreign suppliers;
* bargaining power.

Ask:

```text
If foreign access is restricted, what exactly can this company still do?
Which component, process, material, software, or patent is truly self-controlled?
```

---

## Globalization Narrative

Do not accept:

* “going global”
* “overseas expansion”
* “international layout”

unless it shows:

* overseas revenue;
* overseas profit;
* overseas channel;
* overseas compliance;
* overseas brand;
* local production;
* repeat customers.

Ask:

```text
Is this company exporting products, or building a real overseas operating system?
```

---

## New Quality Productive Forces Narrative

Do not accept:

* policy wording;
* industrial buzzwords;
* conference slogans;
* thematic fund language.

Ask:

```text
What scarce capability does this company actually control?
Can that capability survive competition, policy change, and margin pressure?
```

# 8. Valuation Stress Test

Do not say a company is attractive only because it is a good company.

Test valuation under pressure.

Use these scenarios:

```text
Revenue growth cut by half.
Gross margin drops by 3 percentage points.
Overseas tariffs increase.
AI commercialization is delayed by 2 years.
Domestic price war continues.
US dollar rates remain high.
Export controls tighten.
Capital expenditure rises.
Inventory cycle reverses.
```

Then answer:

```text
If the good story is delayed, does the stock still make sense?
If the company is good but the valuation is too full, what needs to happen before it becomes attractive?
```

````
# 10. Judgment Rules

Use these rules strictly.

## Rule 1: Do not reward slogans.

If a company says it has AI, self-developed technology, globalization, or new-quality productivity, ask where it appears in financial results.

## Rule 2: Do not confuse revenue with power.

Revenue growth is not enough.

Look for:

- margin;
- cash flow;
- customer dependence;
- pricing power;
- supply-chain control.

## Rule 3: Do not confuse export with globalization.

Export is selling abroad.

Globalization is operating abroad.

## Rule 4: Do not confuse patents with technology.

A patent matters only if it protects a product, process, cost advantage, standard, or customer lock-in.

## Rule 5: Do not confuse good company with good investment.

A company may be excellent but too expensive.

## Rule 6: Always identify the failure condition.

Every bullish thesis must include:

```text
What would prove this thesis wrong?
````

## Rule 7: Separate fact, inference, and speculation.

Use labels:

```text
Fact:
Inference:
Speculation:
Unknown:
```

## Rule 8: Prefer hard evidence over narrative.

Evidence hierarchy:

```text
Audited financials > segment revenue > customer contracts > production capacity > verified shipment data > management guidance > media report > investor presentation > slogan
```

The purpose of this skill is not to predict stock prices.

The purpose is to prevent the investor from mistaking:

```text
story for capability,
capability for profit,
profit for cash flow,
good company for good investment,
and national narrative for shareholder return.
```
