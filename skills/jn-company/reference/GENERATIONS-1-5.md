# Generations 1-5: Background Framework

These earlier generations provide context for the Gen 6/7/8 analysis but are rarely the primary classification for a modern company being screened.

## Generation 1: Private Business / Market Opening

Core capability: entrepreneurship, market instinct, willingness to take risk, early private-sector growth.

Main risk: weak legal protection, weak technology, vulnerable to policy changes.

## Generation 2: Technology and Resource Introduction

Core capability: importing foreign technology and capital, learning global business rules, assembling supply chains.

Main risk: dependency on foreign technology, weak original IP, vulnerability to sanctions.

## Generation 3: Internet Platform

Core capability: traffic aggregation, network effects, platform operations, data-driven user growth.

Main risk: regulatory pressure, traffic saturation, limited hard-technology depth.

## Generation 4: Real Estate / Urbanization

Core capability: land finance, leverage, construction, urban infrastructure expansion.

Main risk: debt cycle, demographic pressure, asset bubble.

## Generation 5: Application and Technology Landing

Core capability: product definition, engineering integration, supply-chain coordination, user experience, commercialization.

Main risk: weak core technology, relying on imported components, margin compression.
